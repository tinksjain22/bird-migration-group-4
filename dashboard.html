Wildfire Impact

Wildfire happens all over the globe and is impacted by many different environmental factors such as a lightning strike in a dry area, or human error like uncontrolled burning or even leaving a cigarette out. Wildfire around the globe contributes to severe habitat loss to birds and other animals and also produces carbon monoxide gas, which is a toxic gas. When considering birds, it is clear how wildfires may affect them - visibility may be low due to smoke, birds could get sick and die from carbon monoxide, and there is also a lack of land and vegetation for them to nest and lay eggs.

Here we demonstrate the worldwide distribution of wildfires from 2013 to 2023. In the heatmap on the right, you can see the top 10 countries with the most area burned over the 10 year period.

Taking a closer look at one of the top 10 countries burned between 2013- 2023 (Brazil), we can examine how wildfire impacts bird movement in more detail. Below you can see the number of reported wildfires in Brazil in 2013 and 2017 and how the increase in wildfires in 2017 limits the area in which the Great Kiskadee bird (Pitangus sulphuratus) is viewes in the country.
